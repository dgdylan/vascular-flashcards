Question 17
Missed
Front
Which of the following arteries will be compressed when evaluating frontal artery flow for a periorbital Doppler exam?
Choices
Answer: C. superficial temporal artery and infraorbital artery
Feedback:
A periorbital Doppler exam involves using CW Doppler to evaluate flow in the frontal artery (branch of the ophthalmic artery). Normal flow direction will be toward the probe. Flow moving away from the probe indicates a significant obstruction in the ipsilateral ICA. Compression of the infraorbital, facial and superficial temporal arteries is performed on both sides of the head, while monitoring the frontal artery flow. If compression of one of these arteries causes decreased or reversed flow in the frontal artery, the compressed vessel is being used as a collateral to fill the frontal artery (not the ICA).

Which of the following is a potential collateral pathway for extracranial to intracranial blood flow?
Choices
Answer: D. Facial and maxillary artery branches connect to small branches of the orbital artery
Feedback:
ECA branches provide collateral pathways when stenosis is present in the intracranial system. The superficial temporal artery branches (ECA) connect to small branches of the ophthalmic artery (ICA). Facial and maxillary artery branches (ECA) connect to small branches of the orbital artery (ICA). Tip for collateralization questions: Know your branches! For this question, the key is selecting branches from two different supply lines. ECA and posterior circulation branches that are connected to the intracranial branches will supply flow to a Circle of Willis that is obstructed in an area.

Question 30
Front
Which of the following normally demonstrates a higher resistance Doppler flow profile?
Choices
Answer: A. external carotid artery
Feedback:
"Organ flow" will typically be low resistance and constant flow is needed for the organs to function. The post prandial SMA will be lower resistance with increased diastolic flow due to increased digestive system function. The renal and hepatic arteries will also have low resistance flow with increased diastolic flow because they constantly function. "Extremity flow" is typically high resistance as muscles/tissue need much less inflow, especially at rest. The external carotid artery and its branches feed mainly muscles and tissue, therefore the flow demonstrates a higher resistance waveform.

Choices
Answer: B. Has a low resistance waveform
Feedback:

The ICA should normally demonstrate a low resistance waveform, while the ECA demonstrates a higher resistance waveform.

Question 43

What is occurring on the Doppler tracing where the arrows are pointing?
Choices
Answer: C. Temporal tap
Feedback:
