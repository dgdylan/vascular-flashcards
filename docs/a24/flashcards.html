Choices

Feedback:
The waveform displays mild respiratory variation (phasicity) and pulsatility in a normal renal vein. Please note that the definition of pulsatility indicates regular variations in flow. Not all pulsatility in venous flow is a result of the ripple effects of cardiac contraction or cardiac pulsatility. These findings are considered normal in renal vessels. If venous congestion occurs, the flow will become increasingly pulsatile and flow reversal can develop during atrial contraction.
