Question 5
Front
How can the gastrocnemius veins and the posterior tibial veins be differentiated on an ultrasound exam?
Choices
Answer: C. Follow them distally to locate their point of origination
Feedback:
Gastrocnemius veins originate deep within the calf muscle and extend to the popliteal fossa to join the popliteal vein. The posterior tibial veins originate at the ankle and extend to join the tibioperoneal trunk near the popliteal fossa. If you are progressing down the leg for a venous evaluation, when you reach the popliteal fossa and begin noting the calf vessels, pick a set to follow. If they dive deep into the calf = gastrocnemius veins, if they extend to the ankle = PTVs. Both sets of veins have a corresponding artery and flow moving toward the heart. These characteristics would not differentiate them from each other.
