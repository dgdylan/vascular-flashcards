The waveform from an abdominal vessel displays what two characteristics?
Study Mode
Not answered yet
Answer: B. mild phasicity and pulsatility
Feedback:
The waveform displays mild respiratory variation (phasicity) and pulsatility in a normal renal vein. Please note that the definition of pulsatility indicates regular variations in flow. Question 9
Front
Poiseuille's Law states that the ___________________ has the most significant effect on the blood flow in a vessel.
Study Mode
Not answered yet
Answer: C. vessel radius
Feedback:
Poiseuille's Law demonstrates the relationship between pressure, flow volume and resistance. A small change in vessel radius causes a dramatic change in the blood flow within. Note the equation lists the radius of the blood vessel and it is raised to the 4th power. This is a significant numerical increase (or decrease) with changes to the radius measurement. Tip for these types of questions: Know your equations! memorizing the Bernoulli, Poiseuille's Law, and Doppler equation will be very helpful in answering relationship questions. Characteristics on the top of the division equation are directly related to the outcome number. Characteristics on the bottom of a division equation are indirectly related to the outcome number. For example: Flow volume = Pressure divided by Resistance. Q= P/R As pressure increases, flow volume increases (direct relationship). As resistance increases, flow volume decreases (indirect or inverse relationship).

Question 17
Front
Which of the following terms can be used to describe the normal Doppler waveform in the portal vein?
Study Mode
Not answered yet
Answer: D. mild pulsatility with respiratory variation
Feedback:
Venous flow in the portal system normally demonstrates mild respiratory variation and pulsatility. The increased thickness of the vessel walls and location in the abdomen prevent significant cardiac effects on the flow pattern. Mildly pulsatile flow is considered normal with a pulsatility index less than 30%. The pulsatility is not considered "cardiac pulsatility" as the variation is due to the normal flow reversal from the hepatic veins into the venous sinusoids that slows portal inflow. As hepatic vein flow reversal increases with congestion, the pulsatility of the portal vein will increase to abnormal levels.

Question 45
Front
Which of the following flow characteristics is/are seen in a normal hepatic vein Doppler tracing?
Study Mode
Not answered yet
Answer: D. cardiac pulsatility and respiratory phasicity
Feedback:
Cardiac pulsatility is normal seen in the hepatic veins and the upper abdominal portion IVC. The motion of the heart causes vibrations within the chest that are carried throughout the adjacent structures. The flexibility of the IVC and hepatic veins allow the vibrations to affect the flow inside causing pulsatility. As the diaphragm moves up and down with respiration, the intra-abdominal pressure is affected. Inspiration causes the diaphragm to move down which increases the intra-abdominal pressure. This causes flow in the abdomen and lower extremities to decrease or cease movement. The diaphragm moves upward with expiration and the intra-abdominal pressure decreases allowing flow to move toward the heart again.

Question 58
Front
Where should the Doppler sample be placed to obtain the highest velocity when evaluating laminar flow?
Study Mode
Not answered yet
Answer: C. in the center of the vessel
Feedback:
The Doppler sample should be placed in the center of the vessel to obtain the highest velocity when evaluating laminar flow. The red blood cells (RBCs) in the center of the vessel are moving at relatively the same speed with laminar flow. This results in a waveform with a prominent spectral window. The closer the cursor gets to the wall of the vessel, the greater the friction effect of the RBCs against the wall. This friction causes variation in velocity and increased spectral broadening.

Which of the following normally demonstrates a low resistance Doppler flow profile?
Study Mode
Not answered yet
Answer: B. hepatic artery and renal artery
Feedback:
All vessels that feed organs will have low resistance vascular beds which lead to a low resistance waveform. The external carotid artery and its branches feed mainly muscles and tissue, therefore the flow demonstrates a higher resistance waveform.
