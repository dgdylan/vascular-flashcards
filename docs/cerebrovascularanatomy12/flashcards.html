Show answer and feedback

Answer: C. superficial temporal artery and infraorbital artery

Question 30
Front
Which of the following normally demonstrates a higher resistance Doppler flow profile?
A:external carotid artery
B:post-prandial superior mesenteric artery
C:renal artery
D:hepatic artery
Show answer and feedback
Answer: A. external carotid artery
Feedback:
"Organ flow" will typically be low resistance and constant flow is needed for the organs to function. The post prandial SMA will be lower resistance with increased diastolic flow due to increased digestive system function. The renal and hepatic arteries will also have low resistance flow with increased diastolic flow because they constantly function. "Extremity flow" is typically high resistance as muscles/tissue need much less inflow, especially at rest. The external carotid artery and its branches feed mainly muscles and tissue, therefore the flow demonstrates a higher resistance waveform.
