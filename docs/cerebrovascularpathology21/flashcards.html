Question 3
Front
Which of the following vessels can provide a collateral pathway to redistribute flow from one side of the brain to the other side?
Choices
Answer: D. anterior communicator and basilar artery
Feedback:
The anterior communicating arteries can allow flow to move from one ACA into the other. If the left ICA is occluded, flow from the right ICA enters the brain and travels to the right ACA. Flow then moves through the communicator to fill the left ACA in a retrograde fashion. The cerebellum is supplied by blood from branches of the vertebral arteries and basilar artery. If the left vertebral is occluded proximally, flow moves cephalad toward the brain in the right vertebral artery and then some of this flow is redirected caudally into the left vertebral (retrograde filling).
