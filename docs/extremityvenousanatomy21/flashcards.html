Question 12
Front
When transmural pressure in a vein is high:
A:the associated artery will flatten and flow velocity increases
B:the vein becomes flattened and dumbbell shaped
C:the vein becomes enlarged and rounded
D:the associated artery will distend and flow velocity decreases
Show answer and feedback
Answer: C. the vein becomes enlarged and rounded
Feedback:
Transmural pressure is the pressure within the veins pushing outward and is normally low. It increases with increasing venous volume. When the pressure inside the vein is high compared to the surrounding pressure outside the vein, the vessel distends. When the pressure inside the vein is low compared to the surrounding pressure outside the vein, the vessel collapses to a dumbbell shape.

Question 43
Missed
Front
What venous valves are closed in the lower extremity during calf muscle relaxation?
A:All of the valves in the deep and superficial system
B:Proximal thigh veins
C:Calf veins
D:All of the valves in the deep system
Show answer and feedback
Answer: B. Proximal thigh veins
Feedback:
During muscle contraction, the large blood volume residing in the deep calf veins and soleal sinuses is squeezed up and out of the calf. The proximal valves of the deep veins are forced open. The distal valves close to prevent caudal flow. The perforator valves close to prevent flow into the superficial venous system. During calf muscle relaxation, the proximal valves close due to the hydrostatic pressure forcing blood back down the veins to the level of the valve. During relaxation, the distal and perforator valves open, and blood flows into the calf deep veins.
